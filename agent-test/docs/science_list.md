## Biology

1. **Microscope Cell Lab** — Students observe plant or animal cells under a microscope and identify cell structures.
2. **Cheek Cell Lab** — Students collect cheek cells and view them to study animal cell structure.
3. **Osmosis Potato Lab** — Students place potato slices in different salt solutions to observe water movement.
4. **Yeast Respiration Lab** — Students measure carbon dioxide produced during cellular respiration.
5. **Enzyme Catalase Lab** — Students observe how catalase breaks down hydrogen peroxide.
6. **DNA Extraction Lab** — Students isolate DNA from strawberries or bananas.
7. **Punnett Square Genetics Lab** — Students simulate inheritance patterns using models.
8. **Natural Selection Simulation Lab** — Students model survival advantages using different “traits.”
9. **Bacterial Growth Lab** — Students grow bacteria colonies on agar plates.
10. **Plant Growth Experiment** — Students test how light or nutrients affect plant growth.
11. **Transpiration Lab** — Students measure water loss from plant leaves.
12. **Owl Pellet Lab** — Students dissect owl pellets to identify small animal bones.
13. **Frog Dissection Lab** — Students examine internal organs of a frog to learn anatomy.

## Chemistry

1. **Baking Soda Reaction Lab** — Students observe carbon dioxide production from a simple chemical reaction.
2. **pH Indicator Lab** — Students test acidity and alkalinity of common liquids.
3. **Acid–Base Titration Lab** — Students determine concentration of an acid or base using neutralization.
4. **Density Column Lab** — Students layer liquids of different densities to visualize density differences.
5. **Solubility Lab** — Students test how temperature affects how much solute dissolves.
6. **Precipitation Reaction Lab** — Students mix solutions that produce a solid precipitate.
7. **Flame Test Lab** — Students identify metal ions by flame color.
8. **Reaction Rate Lab** — Students observe how temperature or concentration affects reaction speed.
9. **Gas Law Balloon Lab** — Students observe balloon expansion in hot vs cold environments.
10. **Chromatography Lab** — Students separate pigments from ink or leaves.
11. **Crystal Growth Lab** — Students grow crystals from saturated solutions.
12. **Thermochemistry Lab** — Students measure heat produced or absorbed during reactions.

## Physics

1. **Lens and Optics Lab** — Students investigate how lenses focus light.
2. **Speed and Motion Lab** — Students measure velocity and acceleration using moving carts.
3. **Newton’s Laws Lab** — Students investigate how force affects motion.
4. **Projectile Motion Lab** — Students launch objects and measure their trajectories.
5. **Gravity Lab** — Students measure the acceleration due to gravity.
6. **Friction Lab** — Students measure friction between surfaces.
7. **Pendulum Period Lab** — Students study how pendulum length affects swing time
8. **Energy Conservation Lab** — Students observe conversion between potential and kinetic energy.
9. **Momentum Collision Lab** — Students analyze collisions between carts.
10. **Electric Circuit Lab** — Students build series and parallel circuits.
11. **Electromagnet Lab** — Students create magnets using electricity.
12. **Wave on String Lab** — Students measure wavelength and frequency of waves.

## Earth science

1. Rock Identification Lab — Students classify rocks by appearance and properties.
2. Mineral Hardness Lab — Students determine hardness using scratch tests.
3. Volcano Eruption Model Lab — Students simulate volcanic eruptions using chemical reactions.
4. Water Cycle Model Lab — Students model evaporation and condensation in a closed system.
5. Crater Formation Lab — Students simulate meteor impacts by dropping objects into sand.
6. Fossil Identification Lab — Students examine fossil samples and classify them based on shape and type.
7. Fossil Formation Lab — Students press shells or objects into clay to model how fossils form over time.
8. Glacier Movement Lab — Students use ice over sand to model how glaciers carve landscapes.
9. Continental Drift Puzzle Lab — Students cut and match continent shapes (like South America and Africa) to model Pangaea.

## Environment science

1. Water Quality Testing Lab — Students test water samples for pH, dissolved oxygen, turbidity, and nitrates.
2. Water Filtration Lab — Students build filters using sand, gravel, and charcoal to clean dirty water.
3. Oil Spill Simulation Lab — Students simulate an oil spill and test different cleanup methods.
4. Air Pollution Collection Lab — Students collect airborne particles using sticky cards or filters.
5. Greenhouse Effect Lab — Students compare temperature changes in covered vs uncovered containers under light.
6. Soil Composition Lab — Students separate soil into layers (sand, silt, clay) using water settling.
7. Soil pH Lab — Students test soil acidity and relate it to plant growth.
8. Erosion Lab — Students pour water over soil to observe erosion and land changes.
9. Runoff Pollution Lab — Students simulate rain carrying pollutants into waterways.
10. Biodiversity Survey Lab — Students count species in a local area to measure ecosystem diversity.
11. Food Web Lab — Students model energy flow between organisms in an ecosystem.
12. Population Growth Lab — Students simulate population changes using data or models.
13. Invasive Species Simulation Lab — Students model how invasive species disrupt ecosystems.
14. Habitat Fragmentation Lab — Students simulate how breaking habitats affects species survival.
15. Composting Lab — Students observe decomposition of organic waste over time.
16. Renewable Energy Lab — Students test solar panels or small wind turbines.
17. Albedo Effect Lab — Students compare how different surfaces absorb or reflect heat.
